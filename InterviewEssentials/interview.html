💡 Interview Essentials

Quick tips to help you stay confident and avoid mistakes during your interviews.

  • Always explain your approach before coding.
  • Practice writing code on paper/whiteboard for clarity.
  • Revise time & space complexities regularly.
  • Focus on edge cases: empty input, large input, duplicates.
  • Learn to dry run recursion & DP problems.
  • Master array + binary search — most asked in placements.
  • Be confident in explaining your resume projects.
  • Use hash maps/sets for faster lookups.
  • Speak your thought process while solving — interviewers value clarity.
  • Keep answers structured: intro → approach → solution → edge cases.
  • Binary Search is not just for sorted arrays — learn its variations.

⚠️ Common Mistakes in Interviews

  • Not preparing for basics — ignoring core subjects and focusing only on DSA.
  • Weak project explanation — only naming the project instead of explaining impact.
  • Forgetting time/space complexity — giving correct code but not analyzing efficiency.
  • Lying on resume — adding tools/skills/projects that can’t be explained.
  • Jumping straight to coding — not discussing approach first.
  • Nervous or unprofessional body language.
  • Interrupting the interviewer.
  • Not asking questions at the end.
  • Overusing fillers like “umm”, “like”, “actually”.
  • Poor HR answers — generic statements without examples.

✅ Do’s Before Interview

  • Revise core concepts (OOP, DBMS, OS, CN).
  • Practice telling your project explanation in 2–3 minutes.
  • Dress neatly and professionally.
  • Keep multiple copies of resume + ID ready.
  • Join interview 10 mins early (online).

❌ Don’ts Before Interview

  • Don’t memorize answers word-for-word.
  • Don’t ignore company profile / job description.
  • Don’t interrupt the interviewer while speaking.
  • Don’t forget to test microphone/webcam (for online).
  • Don’t show nervous body language.
